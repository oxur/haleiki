 A **mantle** is a layer inside a [planetary body](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planetary_body "Planetary body") bounded below by a [core](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planetary_core "Planetary core") and above by a [crust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crust_\(geology\) "Crust (geology)"). Mantles are made of [rock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock_\(geology\) "Rock (geology)") or [ices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volatile_\(astrogeology\) "Volatile (astrogeology)"), and are generally the largest and most massive layer of the planetary body. Mantles are characteristic of planetary bodies that have undergone [differentiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planetary_differentiation "Planetary differentiation") by [density](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Density "Density"). All [terrestrial planets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terrestrial_planet "Terrestrial planet") (including [Earth](/source/earth/ "Earth")), half of the [giant planets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giant_planets "Giant planets"), specifically [ice giants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ice_giant "Ice giant"), a number of [asteroids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asteroid "Asteroid"), and some planetary [moons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_satellite "Natural satellite") have mantles.

## Examples

### Earth

![The internal structure of Earth](../media/mantle/Earth_Internal_Structure.svg)The internal structure of Earth

The [Earth's mantle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth's_mantle "Earth's mantle") is a layer of [silicate rock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicate_minerals "Silicate minerals") between the [crust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crust_\(geology\) "Crust (geology)") and the [outer core](/source/earths-outer-core/ "Earth's outer core"). Its mass of 4.01 × 1024 kg is 67% of the mass of the Earth. It has a thickness of 2,900 kilometres (1,800 mi) making up about 84% of Earth's volume. It is predominantly solid, but in [geological time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geologic_time_scale "Geologic time scale") it behaves as a [viscous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viscosity "Viscosity") [fluid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluid "Fluid"). [Partial melting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partial_melting "Partial melting") of the mantle at [mid-ocean ridges](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mid-ocean_ridge "Mid-ocean ridge") produces [oceanic crust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oceanic_crust "Oceanic crust"), and partial melting of the mantle at [subduction zones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subduction_zones "Subduction zones") produces [continental crust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continental_crust "Continental crust").

### Other planets

[Mercury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mercury_\(planet\) "Mercury (planet)") has a silicate mantle approximately 490 kilometers (300 miles) thick, constituting only 28% of its mass. [Venus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venus "Venus")'s silicate mantle is approximately 2,800 kilometers (1,700 miles) thick, constituting around 70% of its mass. [Mars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mars "Mars")'s silicate mantle is approximately 1,600 kilometers (990 miles) thick, constituting ~74–88% of its mass, and may be represented by [chassignite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martian_meteorite "Martian meteorite") meteorites. [Uranus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uranus "Uranus") and [Neptune](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neptune "Neptune")'s ice mantles are approximately 30,000 km thick, composing 80% of both masses.

### Moons

[Jupiter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jupiter "Jupiter")'s moons [Io](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Io_\(moon\) "Io (moon)"), [Europa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europa_\(moon\) "Europa (moon)"), and [Ganymede](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganymede_\(moon\) "Ganymede (moon)") have silicate mantles; Io's ~1,100 kilometers (680 miles) silicate mantle is overlain by a volcanic crust, Ganymede's ~1,315 kilometers (817 miles) thick silicate mantle is overlain by ~835 kilometers (519 miles) of ice, and Europa's ~1,165 kilometers (724 miles) km silicate mantle is overlain by ~85 kilometers (53 miles) of ice and possibly liquid water.

The silicate mantle of the [Earth's moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moon "Moon") is approximately 1300–1400 km thick, and is the source of [mare basalts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moon_rock "Moon rock"). The lunar mantle might be exposed in the [South Pole-Aitken basin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Pole–Aitken_basin "South Pole–Aitken basin") or the [Crisium basin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mare_Crisium "Mare Crisium"). The lunar mantle contains a seismic discontinuity at ~500 kilometers (310 miles) depth, most likely related to a change in composition.

[Titan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titan_\(moon\) "Titan (moon)") and [Triton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triton_\(moon\) "Triton (moon)") each have a mantle made of [ice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ice "Ice") or other [solid volatile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ice#Non-water "Ice") substances.

### Asteroids

Some of the largest [asteroids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asteroid "Asteroid") have mantles; for example, [Vesta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/4_Vesta "4 Vesta") has a silicate mantle similar in composition to [diogenite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diogenite "Diogenite") meteorites.
