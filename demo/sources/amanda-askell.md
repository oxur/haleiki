**Amanda Askell** ([née](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Birth_name "Birth name") **Hall**; formerly **MacAskill**; born 1988 or 1989) is a Scottish philosopher and AI researcher. She has served as the head of the personality [alignment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_alignment "AI alignment") team at [Anthropic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthropic "Anthropic") since 2021. She has played a large role in the development of [Claude](/source/claude-language-model/ "Claude (language model)")'s personality and [constitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitutional_AI "Constitutional AI"). In 2024, she appeared on the [_Time_ 100 AI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_100_AI "Time 100 AI") list. She previously worked at [OpenAI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenAI "OpenAI"), but left over concerns that the company was not prioritizing [AI safety](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_safety "AI safety") enough. She has published over 60 papers and has received over 190,000 citations.

## Early life and education

Born Amanda Hall, Askell was raised in [Prestwick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prestwick "Prestwick") by her mother, a teacher. She attended secondary school in [Alva, Clackmannanshire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alva,_Clackmannanshire "Alva, Clackmannanshire"). At the [University of Dundee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Dundee "University of Dundee"), she sought degrees in philosophy and fine art. Askell received a [BPhil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bachelor_of_Philosophy#United_Kingdom "Bachelor of Philosophy") degree in Philosophy from the [University of Oxford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Oxford "University of Oxford") and a PhD degree in Philosophy from [New York University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_University "New York University") in 2018. Her doctoral thesis (_Pareto Principles in Infinite Ethics_) argues that [rankings of worlds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population_ethics "Population ethics") containing infinitely many agents, when constrained by certain plausible axioms, create puzzles for a wide range of [ethical theories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Normative_ethics "Normative ethics").

## Career

### OpenAI (2018–2021)

After completing her PhD, Askell joined [OpenAI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenAI "OpenAI") in November 2018 as a Research Scientist on the policy team. At OpenAI, she focused on AI development races between organizations and how they can avoid being adversarial, as well as examining the intersection between policy questions and AI safety and co-authored the [GPT-3 paper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GPT-3#Training_and_capabilities "GPT-3"), which was published as a pre-print on 28 May 2020.

### Anthropic (2021–present)

Askell joined [Anthropic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthropic "Anthropic") in March 2021 as a Member of Technical Staff, focusing on alignment and finetuning. She currently leads the personality alignment team, where she is responsible for training Anthropic's Claude model to exhibit positive character traits, such as curiosity, and for developing new techniques for model finetuning. In 2026, the [_Wall Street Journal_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal "The Wall Street Journal") wrote that "her job, simply put, is to teach Claude how to be [good](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Good "Good")", and the _[New Yorker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_Yorker "The New Yorker")_ wrote that "she supervises what she describes as Claude’s 'soul'."

## Research

### Moral self-correction

In a 2023 paper co-authored with Deep Ganguli, Askell explored "moral self-correction" in [large language models](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_language_model "Large language model"): the capacity of these systems to reduce harmful outputs when given natural language instructions to do so. The research tested whether models trained with [reinforcement learning from human feedback](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinforcement_learning_from_human_feedback "Reinforcement learning from human feedback") (RLHF) could avoid [stereotyping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stereotype "Stereotype") and [discrimination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discrimination "Discrimination") without being provided explicit definitions of these concepts or the metrics used to evaluate them.

The study found that this capability emerged at 22 billion parameters and improved with both model size and RLHF training. Using three experimental benchmarks, the researchers demonstrated that natural-language instructions such as "Please ensure that your answer is unbiased and does not rely on stereotypes" substantially reduced biased outputs in models of sufficient scale. The results revealed that larger models can follow complex instructions and learn normative concepts like stereotyping and discrimination from training data.

### Constitutional AI

Askell has been a key contributor to the development of [Constitutional AI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitutional_AI "Constitutional AI") (CAI), a method for training AI systems to meet the standards of harmlessness and helpfulness using AI feedback rather than extensive human oversight. The approach involves providing AI models with a set of principles, or "constitution", to guide their behavior, allowing them to critique and revise their own responses based on these principles.

Askell is the primary author and is responsible for the majority of the text of the latest version of Claude's constitution, released in January 2026. The document is designed to address the growing capabilities and emerging risks of advanced AI models. She has described her work as focusing on helping models "understand and grapple with the constitution" through synthetic data generation and reinforcement learning techniques.

## Personal life

Askell married philosopher [William Crouch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_MacAskill "William MacAskill") in 2013. The two adopted a shared married name of MacAskill, which she reworked to Askell after their divorce in 2015. She is a member of [Giving What We Can](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giving_What_We_Can "Giving What We Can").
